# AgeCraft playtest feedback and next-iteration plan

## What to ask testers right after the run
1. At any point, were you unsure what to do next?
2. Did crafting feel exciting, confusing, or mostly like a means to reach the market?
3. Did you ever feel a meaningful choice between **craft more** vs **buy now**?
4. Which era felt best? Which era felt weakest?
5. Did creator identity / loyalty feel interesting or easy to ignore?
6. If you stopped early, what pushed you out?

## What to observe during play
- time to first meaningful craft
- time to first bottleneck clear
- whether players read the gate checklist or ignore it
- whether players inspect items before buying
- whether they understand why an item failed the gate
- whether they feel shells are scarce in a frustrating way or in a tension-creating way

## Success signals
- players can describe the loop back in their own words
- at least one craft-vs-buy decision feels deliberate
- players can explain what a gate-worthy item looks like
- Bronze and Classical feel like genuine progression, not just recolors
- players notice creator loyalty as more than decorative flavor

## Failure signals
- players spam gather without understanding why
- players buy items blindly because crafting logic feels opaque
- players never feel a difference between eras beyond labels
- loyalty is not remembered or does not affect perceived value
- first-minute confusion is still high despite the checklist

## Next iteration branches
### If loop is fun but pacing is weak
- tune shell income, stockpile thresholds, and market pricing
- add one more interesting craft-side reward before each gate

### If market is interesting but crafting is weak
- make trait/lineage interactions more legible
- add one lightweight craft preview or probability hint

### If crafting is interesting but market is ignored
- increase the usefulness/visibility of creator-made items
- show stronger creator differentiation and notable discoveries

### If all three eras are understandable but shallow
- deepen Bronze and Classical with 1-2 signature mechanics each before touching blockchain reality

## Rule for the next phase
Do not jump into real NFT/on-chain integration until playtest feedback shows the underlying loop is compelling enough to deserve it.
